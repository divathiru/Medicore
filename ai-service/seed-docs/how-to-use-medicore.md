# MediCore Patient Guide — How to Use the Platform

## Overview

MediCore is an online hospital management platform where you can sign up as a patient,
book appointments with specialist doctors, upload your past medical records for AI-assisted
care, and pay your consultation fee before your appointment begins.

This guide answers the most common questions visitors have about using MediCore.

---

## 1. How to Sign Up as a Patient

1. Open the MediCore website in your browser.
2. Click **Get Started** or **Sign Up** on the home page.
3. Fill in the registration form:
   - **Full name** — your legal name as it appears on your ID
   - **Email address** — used to log in; must be unique
   - **Password** — minimum 8 characters
   - **Date of birth** — required for medical record matching
   - **Phone number** — optional, used for appointment reminders
4. Click **Create Account**.
5. You will be taken directly to your patient dashboard.

> Your account is created with the **patient** role automatically. Doctors and cashiers
> have separate accounts managed by hospital administration.

---

## 2. How to Log In

1. Go to the MediCore website and click **Log In**.
2. Enter the email and password you used during sign-up.
3. Click **Sign In**. You will be redirected to your patient dashboard.

If you forget your password, contact the hospital reception to have your account reset.

---

## 3. How to Book an Appointment

1. After logging in, go to **Book Appointment** from your dashboard.
2. Browse the list of available doctors. Each listing shows:
   - Doctor's name and photo
   - Medical department (e.g., Cardiology, Neurology)
   - Consultation fee
   - A short biography
3. Click **Book** next to the doctor you want to see.
4. Select your preferred appointment date from the calendar.
5. Confirm the booking. The appointment status will show as **Booked**.

> **Important:** Booking an appointment does NOT guarantee your queue position.
> You must complete payment at the cashier counter before you are placed in the
> doctor's daily queue. See section 5 (Payment) for details.

---

## 4. How to Upload a Past Medical Summary or Report

Uploading your previous medical records — such as blood test results, discharge
summaries, old prescriptions, or specialist letters — helps your doctor understand
your health history before the consultation, without you needing to explain everything
from scratch.

### How to Upload

1. Log in and open your **Patient Dashboard**.
2. Navigate to the **Medical History** or **Upload Records** section.
3. Click **Upload File** and choose a file from your device.
4. Supported file types: **.pdf**, **.docx**, **.txt**, **.md**
5. The file is uploaded securely to the hospital's private storage.

### What Happens After You Upload

- The uploaded document is automatically processed by MediCore's AI system.
- The AI reads the document, breaks it into meaningful sections, and stores a
  searchable summary in the hospital's private database — linked only to your patient ID.
- Your doctor can use the **Ask AI** feature during your appointment to query this
  summary in plain language (e.g., "What medications was this patient on last year?")
  and get an instant answer drawn from your uploaded records.
- **Your documents are private.** Only doctors who have an active appointment with
  you can access this information. No one else — including other doctors — can see it.

### Tips for Best Results

- Upload the most recent and relevant reports (within the last 1–2 years).
- If you have multiple documents, upload them one at a time.
- Plain text and PDF files are the most reliably extracted.
- Scanned images inside PDFs may not be read correctly — typed/digital PDFs work best.

## 5. How Payment Works Before Seeing the Doctor

MediCore requires payment to be confirmed by the hospital cashier before you are
placed in the doctor's appointment queue for the day.

### Step-by-step

1. After booking your appointment, visit the **hospital cashier counter** on the day
   of your appointment (or call the reception desk).
2. Provide your registered email address or patient ID.
3. The cashier will process your consultation fee payment.
4. Once payment is confirmed in the system:
   - Your appointment status changes from **Booked** → **In Queue**.
   - You are assigned a **queue position number** (e.g., "You are patient #3 today
     for Dr. Amelia Chen").
5. The doctor's dashboard updates automatically to show your position in their daily
   queue.

> Payment can currently be processed at the hospital premises only. Online payment
> integration is planned for a future release.

---

## 6. What Is the "Ask AI" Feature? (For Curious Patients)

When your doctor is reviewing your case, they have access to an **Ask AI** button
next to your patient record. This lets the doctor type a plain-language question —
such as "What was this patient's last prescribed medication?" — and get an instant
answer drawn from the medical documents you uploaded.

It is an AI assistant for your doctor, not a diagnostic tool. It does not make
medical decisions; it only helps the doctor find relevant information from your
records faster.

---

## 7. Frequently Asked Questions

**Can I book more than one appointment?**
Yes. You can book appointments with different doctors or for different dates. Each
appointment is managed separately.

**Can I cancel an appointment?**
Contact the hospital reception to cancel. Cancellation policies depend on how close
the cancellation is to the appointment date.

**Is my data safe?**
Yes. All uploaded files are stored in a private, encrypted cloud storage bucket.
Your medical history is only accessible to doctors with whom you have an active
appointment.

**What if my file format is not supported?**
Convert your document to PDF or plain text (.txt) before uploading. Most word
processors (Microsoft Word, Google Docs) can export to PDF.

**How long does it take for my uploaded document to be processed by the AI?**
Typically within a few seconds to a minute, depending on file size. The processing
happens automatically in the background after you upload.
